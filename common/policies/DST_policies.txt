# The logic for disabling is not completely meta-focused (this prooved to be a lie) but just so that you don't see AI go full global_unrest reduction or num_of_cultures. It's also a case of policies that are not really suited for AI behaviour. For example, stability_cost_modifier policies will be taken by a player and in the same month (if no other modifiers can be stacked) stabbed up. Afterwhich they will remove it as soon as possible. AI on the other hand, might stab up and THEN take the policies. Or worse take them but not stab up at all, resulting in waste. While it is somewhat possible to manufacture player logic into AI with ai_will_do I'd rather save my brain cells for EU5.

# At the end of the day, the priority is for AI to be competent in such a manner that they are a threat to the player. -40% Culture Conversion for AI will not really threaten a player.

# By the way it is only now that I realise how many vanilla policies are also completely disabled for AI, guh damn.
